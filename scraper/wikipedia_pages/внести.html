Old Church Slavonic

[edit]

Verb

[edit]

внести • (vnesti) (East Church Slavonic)

  1. to bring in
    • 1581, Ostrog Bible, Luke 5.18:
      […] ї искахꙋ внести его и положити пред нимъ.
      […] i iskaxu vnesti ego i položiti pred nimŭ.
      […] and they sought means to bring him in, and to lay him before him.
  2. to bring into

Russian

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

в- (v-) +‎ нести́ (nestí)

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [vnʲɪˈsʲtʲi]
  • Audio:(file)

Verb

[edit]

внести́ • (vnestí) pf (imperfective вноси́ть)

  1. to carry in, to bring in
  2. to pay in, to deposit
  3. to bring in / about, to cause, to introduce
    внести́ разла́д в семью́ ― vnestí razlád v semʹjú ― to cause dissension in the family
    внести́ оши́бку ― vnestí ošíbku ― to introduce an error
    внести́ оживле́ние ― vnestí oživlénije ― to brighten up
    внести́ беспоря́док ― vnestí besporjádok ― to cause disorder, to bring about disorder
  4. to enter, to include, to insert
    внести́ усоверше́нствование ― vnestí usoveršénstvovanije ― to introduce an improvement
    внести́ измене́ния ― vnestí izmenénija ― to make alterations / changes
    внести́ попра́вки ― vnestí poprávki ― to insert / introduce amendments (into)
    внести́ в спи́сок ― vnestí v spísok ― to enter in the list, to put on the list, enroll
  5. to move, to bring in, to bring / put forward, to submit, to table
    внести́ законопрое́кт ― vnestí zakonoproékt ― to introduce a bill
  6. (in expressions)
    внести́ я́сность (в де́ло) ― vnestí jásnostʹ (v délo) ― to clarify (a matter)
    внести́ свой вклад ― vnestí svoj vklad ― to make one's contribution
    внести́ свой вклад в де́ло ― vnestí svoj vklad v délo ― to do one's bit

Ukrainian

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • унести́ (unestý)

Etymology

[edit]

From в- (v-) +‎ нести́ (nestý). Compare Russian внести́ (vnestí), Polish wnieść.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [wneˈstɪ]
  • Audio:(file)

Verb

[edit]

внести́ • (vnestý) pf (imperfective вно́сити) (transitive)

  1. to bring in, to carry in
  2. to bring, to contribute, to introduce (to add something)
    Synonym: привнести́ pf (pryvnestý)
  3. to enter (to include in a list, plan, etc.)
    Synonym: вписа́ти pf (vpysáty)
  4. (finance) to contribute, to pay in
  5. (figuratively) to bring, to bring about (to cause, to occasion)
  6. to bring forward, to put forward (:proposal)
