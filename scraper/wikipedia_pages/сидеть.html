Russian

[edit]

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • сидѣ́ть (sidě́tʹ) — pre-1918 spelling

Etymology

[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic сѣдѣти (sěděti), from Proto-Slavic *sěděti, from Proto-Balto-Slavic *sēdḗˀtei, from Proto-Indo-European *sed-.

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [sʲɪˈdʲetʲ]
  • Audio:(file)
  • Homophones: седе́ть (sedétʹ)

Verb

[edit]

сиде́ть • (sidétʹ) impf (perfective сесть or посиде́ть, verbal noun сиде́ние)

  1. to sit
  2. (colloquial) to be imprisoned, to do time
  3. to stay at home (with), to look after [with с (s, + instrumental) ‘someone’]
  4. to stay (in a physical location)
    сиде́ть до́ма ― sidétʹ dóma ― to stay home
  5. (colloquial) to stay (in a virtual location by using something computer-based)
    сиде́ть в Интерне́те ― sidétʹ v Internéte ― to stay on the Internet
    сиде́ть в ча́те ― sidétʹ v čáte ― to stay in a chat
    сиде́ть на са́йте ― sidétʹ na sájte ― to stay on a website
  6. to use a tool or be occupied with, to sit at (usually in a sitting position) [with за (za, + instrumental) ‘an activity’]
    сиде́ть за компью́тером ― sidétʹ za kompʹjúterom ― to sit at the computer
    сиде́ть пе́ред телеви́зором ― sidétʹ péred televízorom ― to sit before the television
    сиде́ть за́ рабо́той ― sidétʹ zá rabótoj ― to sit at work
