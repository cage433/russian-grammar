Russian

[edit]

Etymology 1

[edit]

Inherited from Old East Slavic писати (pisati, “to write; to paint”), from Proto-Slavic *pьsati. Related to Latin pingō (whence English paint and picture).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [pʲɪˈsatʲ]
  • Audio:(file)

Verb

[edit]

писа́ть • (pisátʹ) impf (perfective написа́ть, verbal noun писа́ние)

  1. to write
    • 1846, Фёдор Достоевский [Fyodor Dostoevsky], “Апреля 8.”, in Бедные люди; English translation from Constance Garnett, transl., Poor People, 1914:
      Вы говори́те, что у вас глаза́ слабе́ют, так не пиши́те при свеча́х; заче́м писа́ть?
      Vy govoríte, što u vas glazá slabéjut, tak ne pišíte pri svečáx; začém pisátʹ?
      You say your eyes are weak; so you must not write by candlelight; why write?
  2. (literary) to paint (a painting)

Etymology 2

[edit]

Borrowed from German pissen, from Old French pissier, from Vulgar Latin *pissiāre, of echoic origin.

Alternative forms

[edit]
  • пи́сять (písjatʹ) — childish or less crude colloquial

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [ˈpʲisətʲ]

Verb

[edit]

пи́сать • (písatʹ) impf (perfective попи́сать)

  1. to urinate, to pee, to piss
