Russian

[edit]

Etymology

[edit]

Inherited from Proto-Slavic *terti.

Cognate with Latin terō (“to rub”), Ancient Greek τρίβω (tríbō, “to rub”), Lithuanian trinti (“to rub”), Latvian trīt (“to rub”), Armenian թրել (tʻrel, “to knead”).

Pronunciation

[edit]
  • IPA(key): [tʲɪˈrʲetʲ]
  • Audio:(file)

Verb

[edit]

тере́ть • (terétʹ) impf (perfective потере́ть)

  1. to rub
  2. to polish
  3. to rub sore, to chafe, to abrade
  4. to grate, to grind
